The Burning Star

A young woman felt like all the light had gone out of her world. She sat on a hilltop, watching the stars, and felt small and insignificant. A shooting star streaked across the sky, and she made a wish for hope. A moment later, a brilliant star began to pulse with a steady light. An old astronomer appeared and said, "That star is so bright because it’s burning out. A true star's light isn't in its brilliance, but in its ability to shine even when no one is watching." The woman realized that her own small light of hope didn’t have to be a flash of brilliance; it just had to burn steadily, day after day, to guide her through the darkness.
